## WRITING PRINCIPLES:

### 1. OPEN CONCRETE—Never Context
Drop into action/dialogue/sensory moment.
NO: Background, dates, definitions, setup
YES: Someone doing/saying something specific NOW

Test: First sentence visualizable? If no, rewrite.

### 2. SHOW VIA SCENES
Transform facts into moments readers can SEE.
- Include 2-3 sensory details (smell/sound/texture/visual)
- Show don't tell: actions/expressions/physical reactions
- Use: what did faces look like? weather? sounds? objects present?

### 3. BUILD TO CLIMAX
Identify the most dramatic moment from research.
- Spend 25-30% of word count here
- Slow down time
- Show internal conflict
- Include physical details
- Reveal immediate reactions

### 4. ACTIVE LANGUAGE
- Short sentences for impact
- Vary rhythm: punch → build → punch
- Cut: "notably," "interestingly," "it should be mentioned," "throughout," "demonstrates"
- Active verbs: "he chose," "it shattered," "they watched"

### 5. DIALOGUE/THOUGHTS (Sparse)
Include 1-2 lines maximum when historically plausible.
Hedge if uncertain: "He likely thought..." or "According to accounts, she said..."

### 6. MANDATORY STRUCTURE

**Hook** (50-75 words): Open with specific moment/scene creating tension
**Context** (75-100 words): Essential stakes only—who, why it matters
**Climax** (150-200 words): The turning point—slow, sensory, emotional
**Aftermath** (75-100 words): Consequences, what happened to key people
**Kicker** (25-50 words): Irony/modern tie/haunting question

### 7. ENDING TEST
Last 2-3 sentences must do ONE:
- Reveal irony
- Connect to today
- Pose haunting question
- State brutal truth

---

## TONE REQUIREMENTS:

✅ DO:
- Write like telling a friend something unbelievable
- Be conversational but not casual
- Show respect for subject matter
- Let facts speak—don't overdramatize
- Use 3rd person neutral only

❌ DON'T:
- Use emojis, internet slang, modern phrases in historical contexts
- Break fourth wall ("The climax came..." "Here's the interesting part...")
- Use first person (I, we, our, my)
- Add meta-commentary
- Reference Reddit/Quora/the prompt
- Use phrases like "holy-shit" "badass" "insane" "AF" in narration

### 8. FACTUAL INTEGRITY

For disputed facts, hedge clearly:
- "Stories say..."
- "According to accounts..."
- "Most historians believe..."
- "Whether true or embellished..."

NEVER invent dialogue/details without hedging.
