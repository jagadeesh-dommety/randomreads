The Abandoned Sequel: Tolkien began a sequel to LOTR called The New Shadow, set 100 years later, but stopped after 13 pages because it felt too "sinister and depressing".
The "Belladonna" Theory: Fans speculate Bilbo's mother, Belladonna Took, was a nod to the hallucinogenic plant; Tolkien reportedly "took belladonna" to spark some of his more surreal visions.
Vatican Latin Protest: A staunch traditionalist, Tolkien was known to loudly shout the Latin Mass over the priest's English translations after the Vatican changed the liturgy.
The Arctic Party: Tolkien and his friend C.S. Lewis once attended a non-costume party dressed as polar bears, just for the amusement of it.
The Ax-Wielding Neighbor: He once dressed as an Anglo-Saxon warrior and chased a neighbor down the street while brandishing a real axe.
The "Mathom" Code: Every source-code file for the Perl programming language starts with a Tolkien quote about "mathoms"—things hobbits have no use for but can't throw away. 
Hidden Lore & Book Easter Eggs

Treebeard's Voice: The booming, slow voice of Treebeard was partially modeled after the eccentric speech patterns of Tolkien's friend C.S. Lewis.
Aragorn’s True Age: Because of his Númenórean blood, Aragorn is actually 87 years old during the events of The Fellowship of the Ring [User Provided].
Industrial Dread: The "Scouring of the Shire" was Tolkien's direct commentary on the industrialization that destroyed the English countryside of his youth.

The Real Broken Toe: When Aragorn kicks the helmet in The Two Towers, Viggo's scream was genuine—he had just broken two toes.

The Female Rohirrim: Most of the Riders of Rohan were actually women with prosthetic beards, chosen because New Zealand had more skilled female horse riders available.
McKellen’s Lone Acting: Due to forced perspective techniques, Ian McKellen (Gandalf) often filmed alone on a separate set, acting toward "stick figures," which reportedly drove him to tears of frustration.

Gollum Juice: Andy Serkis drank a concoction of honey, lemon, and ginger (which he called "Gollum Juice") to protect his throat from the strain of the voice.
The Starlit Eyes: To give Galadriel her "starlight" eyes, the crew built a special lighting rig made of hundreds of Christmas lights.
Viggo’s Horse: Viggo Mortensen became so attached to his horse, Brego, that he bought the animal after filming ended.
The Billion-Link Armor: Two crew members spent two years hand-making over a million chainmail links by hand for the film's armor.
The Post-Oscar Shoot: Peter Jackson was still filming scenes for the Return of the King Extended Edition a month after the movie had already swept the Oscars.
The Minefield Set: The Battle of the Black Gate was filmed on an active military training ground in New Zealand that had to be cleared of unexploded mines first.

The Weinstein Orc: As a "revenge" move after creative disputes, an Orc in the films was specifically modeled after producer Harvey Weinstein.
